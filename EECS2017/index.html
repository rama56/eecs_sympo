EECS Research Students Symposium - 2017

April 07-08, 2017

Venue: Faculty Hall, Indian Institute of Science




EECS 2017 Symposium

Welcome to the Eighth Joint Research Students Symposium of the Departments of CDS, CSA, ECE, EE, and ESE at the Indian Institute of Science!

EECS-2017 primarily is a forum for presentations by senior research students (expecting to graduate in 2017) involving the Departments of Computational and Data Sciences (CDS), Computer Science and Automation (CSA), Electrical Communication Engineering (ECE), Electrical Engineering (EE), and Electronic Systems Engineering (ESE).

        

Keynote Speakers and Special Invitees

M. Annadurai
Director, ISRO Satellite Centre (ISAC), Bangalore.



Future Satellites and Technologies
Friday : 10:15 - 11:00

Abstract

In the modern era of Science & Technology, Satellites have become an integral part of National development. The benefits of satellites are exponentially increasing and its usage has become imperative in every walk of life for a common man. Towards this, the Future Satellite Programme is highly challenging and demands for the realisation of 12-18 Satellites per annum covering a wide spectrum of application areas like Communication, Meteorology, Navigation, Remote Sensing & Interplanetary missions with advanced technology demonstrations.

The Communication satellite programme primarily focuses on bridging the gap of demand Vs capability of huge transponder requirements through High Throughput Satellite(HTS). The Advanced HTS is capable of handling 10-15 KW power to meet the country’s requirements in the communication sector. Further, advanced technologies like Electric propulsion system, I-6k bus developments, LEON 3 processor developments , large power handling systems, 18m unfurlable antenna etc are some of the critical technologies that are being pursued to provide a High quality communication service.

The Ever increasing demands for Remote Sensing Satellite calls for the need of advanced technology demonstration like miniaturisation, onboard computer, onboard autonomy, advanced memory modules and data handling systems , large optics development etc in the Optical & Microwave Imaging domains for national natural resource management. Notwithstanding this, the programme also focuses on building sub-metre resolution satellites to meet the needs of varied user segments. Further the centre is also working on building GEO imaging Satellite that will be a technology breakthrough in the Remote sensing area catering to varied thematic application sectors for country’s development. Navigation is another important domain wherein the continuations of 7 satellite NAVIC constellation are being carried out. The NAVIC Constellation has also provided a platform for the participation of Industries in Satellite building Activities. The centre is marching towards realisation of next generation of navigation satellites with enhanced position accuracy services.

Over and above, the spirit of scientific temper and a move towards exploration of outer space is in full swing with the challenging Lander & Rover technologies, space docking experiments, interplanetary mission studies, XpoSat Mission, Aditya L1 Missions etc on the anvil. Critical scientific instrument developmental activities are being carried out jointly with the active participation of Science & Research Institutes

The decadal achievements of Satellite programme has matured and the entire Satellite programme is undergoing a phenomenal change with a dual focus on Research & Development Activities as well as to scale up the satellite building capacity through active participation of Indian Industries in space sector.The lecture will highlight potential areas of Research both in short term basis and long term basis.

Bio

Dr. Annadurai is currently Director, ISRO Satellite Centre (ISAC), Bangalore.

Dr Annadurai graduated in Electronics and Communication Engineering from Government College of Technology, Coimbatore, obtained his Post Graduate degree in Applied Electronics from the PSG College of Technology, Coimbatore and Doctoral degree from the Anna University.

Joining ISRO in 1982, Dr Annadurai began his career at ISAC as the team leader to design and develop Software Satellite Simulator. During the period 1992 -2005, Dr Annadurai was the lead member of ISRO's satellite mission team and managed eight INSAT Missions as the Mission Director and brought about the efficient ground automation for satellite operations. As Project Director, Dr Annadurai made the most crucial contribution to the realisation of India's first Lunar Mission, Chandrayaan-1, which won many awards including the prestigious Space Pioneer Award, 2009. In 2011, Dr Annadurai became the Programme Director of Indian Remote Sensing Satellites (IRS) and Small Satellite Systems (SSS) and realised five satellite projects. His other major contribution is the realisation of the successful Mars Orbiter Mission in record time.

Notable among the numerous awards and honours received by Dr. Annadurai, are the ISRO Team Excellence award-2007, ISRO Merit Award-2009, ISRO Team excellence award-2010, ISRO Outstanding Achievement Award-2014, National Aeronautical Award-2008 from Aeronautical Society of India, Rajyotsava Award for Science (2008) from Government of Karnataka, Hari Om Ashram Prerit Vikram Sarabhai Research Award (2004), C.P.A. Adithanar Literary Award 2013, Vivekananda Human Excellence Award, 2014 from Ramakrishna Mission, HK Firodia Award 2007, Sri Chandrasekharendra Saraswati National Eminence Award, 2009 for Science and Technology, American Institute of Aeronautics and Astronautics Space Systems Award, 2009, Certificate of Appreciation from Boeing Asia-America Professional Association, USA and Laurels for Team Achievement - Chandrayaan-1 from International Academy of Astronautics, 2013, Beijing. Five universities have conferred Doctor of Science on him.

Dr Annadurai is a Fellow of International Astronautical Federation, Fellow of The Society for Shock Wave Research and a member of International Lunar Network Enabling Technologies Group. He has been awarded the prestigious Padma Shri, 2016 for his contributions in the field of Science & Engineering by the President of India.

How Data Science, Machine Learning, and AI are Transforming the Consumer Experience

During the last two decades the experience of consumers has been undergoing a fundamental and dramatic transformation - giving a rich variety of informed choices, online shopping, consumption of news and entertainment on the go, and personalized shopping experiences. Vinay Kulkarni
Chief Scientist and Head of Software Systems Research, Tata Consultancy Services, Pune, India



Toward a compliant, adaptive, and model driven digital enterprise
Friday : 15:45 - 16:30

Abstract

Modern enterprises face an unprecedented regulatory regime. The cost of compliance continues to grow with traditional compliance practices relying heavily on domain experts whose judgement determines what compliance means and how to reflect regulations onto the enterprise processes and data to make them compliant. Compliance management needs to be automated to the extent possible, so that compliance can be proved quickly, reliably, and maintained through time- and geography-specific variations - while keeping the business efficient. The talk outlines an end-to-end regulatory compliance architecture and associated automation machinery.

Effective decision-making of modern organisation requires deep understanding of various aspects of organisation such as its goals, structure, business-as-usual operational processes, etc. The large size and complex structure of organisations, socio-technical characteristics, and fast business dynamics make decision-making a challenging endeavour. The state-of-practice relying heavily on human experts is turning out to be ineffective, imprecise, based on short-term implications and lacking in agility. The state-of-the-art is also found inadequate. The talk outlines a novel line of attack to the challenging problem of complex dynamic decision making.

Modern enterprises are complex systems of systems characterised by multiple stakeholders with possibly conflicting objectives, increasingly dynamic environment with a set of diverse change-drivers, and constant pressure to maximize growth opportunities while reducing “run-the-business” and “change-the-business” costs. Purely human-centric approach relying on expertise and past experience has turned out to be inadequate with the siloed nature of enterprises further exacerbating the situation. Several architectures such as sense-n-respond, MAPE (for Model, Analyse, Process and Execute) etc have been proposed but found ineffective due largely to absence of sophisticated technological support. Pervasive adoption of software systems that has led to digitization of enterprises offers an opportunity of which the talk provides a bird’s eye view.

Bio

He is a member of the TCS Corporate Technology Council that oversees all R&D and innovation activities at TCS. His research interests include model-driven software engineering, self-adaptive systems, and enterprise modeling. His work in model-driven software engineering has led to a toolset that has been used to deliver several large business-critical systems over the past 20 years. Vinay has served as the conference and program chairperson for the premier ACM and IEEE international conferences in the area of software engineering; and is on technical program committees of many international conferences. Recently, Vinay got inducted as Fellow of Indian National Academy of Engineering. An alumnus of Indian Institute of Technology Madras, Vinay also serves as Visiting Professor at Middlesex University, London.

Siddharth Barman
Department of Computer Science and Automation, IISc.


Finding Equilibrium
Saturday : 11:45 - 12:05

Abstract

Algorithmic game theory is an active and impactful area of research that in recent years has found many applications in the study of large strategic environments, such as online markets and auctions. A basic message that emerged from this area is in fact negative: determining game-theoretic solution concepts---e.g., Nash equilibria---is computationally hard in general. But, given that such solution concepts are widely used in the design and analysis of strategic environments, these complexity barriers need to be addressed. Motivated by these considerations, in this talk I will present research directions that address hardness results for Nash equilibria, which are one of the most well-studied solution concepts in game theory. In particular, I will show that for a relevant class of two-player games an approximate Nash equilibrium can be efficiently determined. A key technical component of this work is an approximate version of Caratheodory's theorem; given the fundamental importance of Caratheodory's theorem, this approximate version is interesting in its own right.

Bio

Siddharth Barman is an Assistant Professor of Computer Science and Ramanujan Fellow at the Indian Institute of Science. Siddharth's research interests lie in the design, analysis, and applications of algorithms. His current research focuses on algorithmic game theory and approximation algorithms. Siddharth received his Ph.D. from the University of Wisconsin - Madison and held a postdoctoral position at the California Institute of Technology.

Kausik Majumdar
Department of Electrical Communication Engineering, IISc.


Externally Controlling Electronic Structure In Two Dimensional Monolayers
Friday : 14:30 - 14:50

Abstract

Two dimensional layers of transition metal dichalcogenides (TMDs) exhibit strong optical activity in spite of their ultra-thin physical thickness. This has generated tremendous interest in two dimensional optoelectronic devices. The exciton binding energy is extremely high (0.3-0.6 eV) in these monolayers due to the out of plane carrier confinement, large carrier effective mass and small dielectric constant. Screening due to surrounding dielectric medium reshapes the electron-hole interaction potential and plays a pivotal role in deciding the binding energies of these strongly bound exciton complexes. However, owing to strong quasi-particle bandgap renormalization in such systems, a direct quantification of estimated shifts in binding energy in different dielectric media remains elusive using optical studies. I shall show that by changing the dielectric environment, one can obtain a large photoluminescence (PL) peak shift at low temperature for higher energy excitons (2s, 3s, 4s, 5s) in monolayer MoSe2, while the 1s exciton peak position remains unaltered - a direct evidence of varying compensation between screening induced exciton binding energy modulation and quasi-particle bandgap renormalization. The estimated modulation of binding energy for the 1s exciton is found to be 58.6% (70.5% for 2s, 78.9% for 3s, 85% for 4s) by coating an Al2O3 layer on top, while the corresponding reduction in quasi-particle bandgap is estimated to be around 0.25 eV. An example of the implications of such band structure change will be discussed in a real device application.

Bio

Dr. Majumdar is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Electrical Communication Engineering at Indian Institute of Science. He works broadly on semiconductor devices with a focus on the physics of novel material systems and their applications in optoelectronics and nano-electronics.

Prasanta Kumar Ghosh
Department of Electrical Engineering, IISc.


Smart Photoplethysmogram For Cuff-less Blood Pressure Measurement
Friday : 18:00 - 18:20

Abstract

Blood pressure (BP) is one of the vital signs that indicate current physical functioning of the body. BP is the measurement of the pressure or force of blood against the walls of arteries. Sphygmomanometer is typically used to measure systolic and diastolic blood pressure by increasing and gradually releasing the pressure in an inflatable rubber cuff applied to the arm. In this presentation, I shall talk about measuring BP using an estimated photoplethysmogram from a video of fingertip taken by the camera of a smartphone, referred to as smart photoplethysmogram. I shall summarize the challenges and present initial results which suggest that the smart photoplethysmogram has the potential for cuff-less BP monitoring.

He received his Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering from University of Southern California (USC), Los Angeles, USA in 2011. Prior to that he obtained his M.Sc.(Engineering) in Electrical Communication Engineering from Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Bangalore and B.E.(ETCE) in Electronics from Jadavpur University, Kolkata in 2006 and 2003 respectively. Hardik J. Pandya
Department of Electronic Systems Engineering, IISc.


Towards a Portable Multi-Parameter Cancer Diagnostic Device
Saturday : 12:05 - 12:25

Abstract

According to World Health Organization (WHO), cancer remains a global health problem and around 14.1 million new cancer cases were diagnosed in 2012 out of which 8.2 million people died. Breast cancer continues to be second largest cause of cancer-related female deaths in the world accounting for 12% amongst all cancer. Despite significant technological advances, screening and intraoperative procedures have remained largely unchanged for decades. The mechanical and electrical phenotyping has been demonstrated as a promising technique to study the progression of the breast cancer. Thus, a device capable of measuring alteration in mechanical, thermal, and electrical properties of the breast tissue quickly, accurately, and simultaneously will potentially open new avenues for breast cancer diagnosis. The design and fabrication of a Portable Multi-Parameter Cancer Diagnostic Device using 3D printing and micro-electro-mechanical-systems (MEMS) technology is presented. The novelty of this work is the simultaneous measurement of multiple tissue parameters, which can provide a deterministic and quantitative information of the tissue characteristics, including mechanical, electrical, and thermal characteristics of the normal tissue from the onset through disease progression of the tissue.

Dr. Hardik J. Pandya is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Electronic Systems Engineering, Division of Electrical Sciences. He works on the integration of biology/medicine with microtechnology, nanotechnology and additive manufacturing to develop innovative tools to solve unmet clinical problems. His current research focuses on flexible sensors, microsensors, microfluidic devices, and microelectromechanical systems, all lately with an emphasis on cancer diagnosis, therapeutics, e-nose, and biomedical device technologies. Before joining IISc, he worked as a postdoctoral scientist in the Department of Mechanical Engineering, Maryland Robotics Center, University of Maryland, College Park and in the Department of Medicine, Brigham and Women’s Hospital–Harvard Medical School affiliated with Harvard-MIT Health Science and Technology.
